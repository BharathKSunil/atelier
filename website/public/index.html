The problem

A wedding — or a year of phone photos — is thousands of frames.
You want the few worth keeping.

TB

Too big to upload

A shoot, a trip, or a whole family archive can be terabytes of photos and bursts. Pushing that to a web service is a painstaking, days-long task — so Atelier never asks you to. It indexes the folder where it already lives.

∿

Bursts, not photos

You didn't take 6,000 photos — you took 900 moments, six frames each. The job isn't rating every frame; it's finding the one frame per moment where nobody blinked. Atelier groups the burst and scores the whole frame, group-aware.

⌘

Never touch the originals

No renames. No moves. No edits. Your selects live in a per-project SQLite database; export only ever copies a chosen original into a new folder. The library you came in with is the library you leave with.

The pipeline

Four passes, then you browse.

Point Atelier at a folder. It indexes once — resumable, offline after the first model download — then you review in the browser at localhost:5050.

  1. Index

    Detect & embed

    RetinaFace detection + ArcFace 512-d face embeddings, quality-gated and EXIF-upright, plus a DINOv2 scene embedding and full-res sharpness / exposure. Resumable.

  2. People

    Cluster into persons

    HDBSCAN over the face embeddings with a centroid-merge pass turns thousands of faces into a handful of people you can name.

  3. Series

    Group the bursts

    EXIF capture time + scene embedding stitch near-identical frames into a single burst, so the keeper-pick is made across the right set.

  4. Score

    Pick the keeper

    One MediaPipe pass reads per-eye blink, head pose, gaze and a genuine-vs-posed smile; numpy reads sharpness, exposure, colour and composition. Each frame is then scored per intent — group photos stay eyes-strict, candids let a great moment beat a blink — so the keeper per burst rises to the top.

What's in the box

A culling tool, not a viewer.

Every frame is read on dozens of signals — the strongest become picks, the telling ones become flags you can trust.

◍

By person — and by pair

A people sidebar that scrolls clean through thousands of faces. Rename, merge, split — or reject a stray face and let Atelier surface the others likely mis-filed with it. Then review by who's in frame: solo, with others, or two together.

∿

Burst-aware picks

Frames gathered back into the burst they came from — by capture time and scene — then six keeper picks: group, everyone, smile, candid, moment, aesthetic. Switch on the ones you cull by.

◉

Reads the whole frame

Per-eye blink, head pose, gaze and a Duchenne smile from one MediaPipe pass; sharpness, exposure, colour and composition in numpy. Plain tags — "7/8 eyes open", "eye contact" — and honest flags for the blown, tilted, busy or near-duplicate.

⚖

Context-aware, not brittle

A blink no longer nukes a frame. Group photos weight eyes strictly; moments stay soft, so a real moment beats a technically-clean dud. Every pick shows its reasons.

◑

Tunable, in plain words

Eleven dials in human language — detection confidence, face size, how tight a cluster, how close a burst. Change one and only the passes it touches re-run; your names and picks ride along.

✦

Learns your taste

Rate the auto-picks, or just keep what you keep — a local model learns from your own choices and re-ranks. Warm-started from the heuristic, so it only ever helps. Nothing leaves the machine.

▤

Buckets & print list

The print list is just your default bucket — Space drops the keeper in. Make your own ("Socials", "Album", "Reject"); a photo lives in many. Tag from the desk, or drop everyone a person is in with one click.

⤓

Portable, private projects

Each project is one SQLite file with thumbnails baked in. Export a self-contained .atelier bundle and open it on another machine; pin, archive, or delete. Originals never move.

↻

Resumable runs

Index a 20,000-frame shoot off an external drive and walk away — it picks up where it stopped, parks a corrupt file instead of crashing, and a clean-up re-run keeps every pick. Per-phase progress and logs stream live; the history survives a restart.

Faces, sorted

Everyone, on one shelf.

insightface — SCRFD/RetinaFace detection with ArcFace embeddings — separates identities far better than the old MTCNN+FaceNet stacks (same-person cosine ~0.98 vs different ~0.15). Fewer junk clusters, fewer jewelry-and-hands false positives.

Tick a face, choose Merge into… or Split out. Your call outlives the algorithm: every manual fix is re-applied after the next HDBSCAN run.

Quickstart

Clone, make start, done.

The web UI is project-centric: create a project, choose a folder with the native picker, and it indexes live.

atelier — zsh
make install     # venv (mise) + full pipeline deps
make start       # web server → http://localhost:5050
make open        # open the dashboard
#   + New Project → Choose Folder → it indexes live
make demo        # or seed a synthetic project to explore first
macOS · Apple SiliconPrimary. CoreML / MPS acceleration, native picker, Reveal in Finder.
macOS · IntelSupported. CPU / CoreML; slower without MPS.
LinuxBest-effort. Pipeline + UI on CPU/CUDA; paste a folder path instead of the native picker.
WindowsUntested — not currently supported.
